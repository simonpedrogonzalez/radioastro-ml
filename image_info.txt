Information about the image

Setup (applies to all panels)

Source J1822–0938 (7.5 hours of observation)
Single spectral window 4.54–4.66 GHz

Figure: fractional residuals under different corruption scenarios (the fraction of brightness that remains unexplained after CLEANing the corrupted visibilities.)

Panels:
1. Thermal noise corruption: additive gaussian noise used to represent thermal noise generated from instrumental sensitivity.
2. Tropospheric corruption: phase distortions caused by atmospheric water vapor fluctuations (considerable windspeed and precipitation used for this example).
3. Single-antenna gain drift: gain instability in only one antenna.
4. Partial array gain drift (7 antennas (25% of the array) affected)
5. Combined corruption (thermal noise + troposphere + partial gain drift). An scenario with all the previously shown effects simultaneously.
